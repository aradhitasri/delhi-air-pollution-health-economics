Economic Cost of Air Pollution on Delhi's Healthcare Infrastructure

Funded by- Center for the Advanced Study of India (CASI), 
University of Pennsylvania  
Researcher-Aradhita Srivastava

Project-
This project estimates the causal economic impact of air pollution 
on healthcare utilization and infrastructure in Delhi, India, using 
econometric regression analysis and original survey data.

Data-
- Administrative health records from Rajya Sabha
- Original survey data collected from 200+ structured interviews 
  across socioeconomic groups in Delhi
  


- `01_graphs.R` — merges all the datasets to construct graphs form the variables as per the findings 
- `02_Worked final 3.R` — Estimates OLS and fixed-effects 
  regressions of pollution exposure on healthcare outcomes; includes 
  robustness checks and sensitivity analysis across socioeconomic subgroups

## Key Findings
Analysis identifies approximately $120 million in annual economic 
burden from pollution-related illness, with heterogeneous effects 
across income groups.
